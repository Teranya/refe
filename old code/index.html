the reflective designers toolkit

Gain a better understanding of your design space

As designers we use reflective thinking in order to understand:

01/ The connections between creativity, criticality and reflection

02/ The importance of curiosity, experimentation and risk taking in design education

03/ Life at University and responsibilities towards undergraduate learning, including self-regulation, motivation and management

04/ Experiential learning, including active participation, research, and critical reflection on your own learning.

Why use the tool:kit.

Reflecting as a design student.

Helps anchor theory in meaningful, concrete experience, helping to bring it alive
Gives recognition to learning gained in non-academic contexts

Structured reflection.

Structured reflection involves identifying significant events that lead to learning, identifying the significant events that lead to learning, identifying their value and considering how they'll contribute to your future projects and practice as a whole.

Building a reflective habit

Reflect and remix.

Building a reflective habit and mindset typically combines at least three key elements:
An ability and willingness to reflect on individual experiences to gain more learning from them.
Repeating reflection over a series of related experiences, building the learning and value each time we go through the reflective cycle.
Regularly reflecting over a longer timeframe to see patterns and opportunities for learning that we may have missed.

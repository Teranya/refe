Why use the tool:kit.

Reflecting as a design student.

Helps anchor theory in meaningful, concrete experience, helping to bring it alive
Gives recognition to learning gained in non-academic contexts

Structured reflection.

Structured reflection involves identifying significant events that lead to learning, identifying the significant events that lead to learning, identifying their value and considering how they'll contribute to your future projects and practice as a whole.

Reflecting on practice

In collaborations, it is essential to be able to articulate as much as you can about this process, about how you practice, so that you can work with others productively… and reflect collectively. In addition, reflection on practice

  • Anchors theory in meaningful, concrete experience, helping to bring it alive;

  • Gives recognition to learning gained in non-academic contexts;

  • Provides a bridge between practical experience and academic study;

  • Helps develop understanding of difficult work situations, improving professional practice.


How to use the tool:kit.

Step 1.

Pick a framework.
  • Choose from a variety of different frameworks, the frameworks all vary in coomplexity
  • Each framework comes with a range of prompt questions.
  • Confused where to start? Start with the DIEP or dive in with The Reflective Framework

Step 2.

Pick a method.
  • Pick a method of doing your reflection
  • Stick with a comfortable style, or try something new
  • Get your materials for the method, could be your laptop, your journal, or another person.

Step 3.

Reflect and remix.
  • Follow the steps in the framework.
  • Or remix the framwork to fit your reflection needs.
